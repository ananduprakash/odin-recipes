Beef Fry

About Beef Fry

Beef fry is a dish made of beef, slow-roasted in a mixture of spices, onions, curry leaves, and coconut slivers, fried in coconut oil. The dish is also popularly known in Kerala as "Beef Ullarthiyathu".

The dishes origins can be traced back to AD 52, after which Syrian Christians have been known to have settled in Kerala, however the first Jewish settlers arrived in Kerala as early as 6th century BC, and brought with them Kosher cattle slaughter techniques, which could have also led to the development of the recipe.

Ingredients

  • Thinly sliced coconut - ½ a cup
  • Shallots cut into two - 1 cup
  • Coriander powder - 3 tbsp
  • Chilli powder - 2 tsp
  • Black pepper powder - 2 tsp
  • Turmeric powder - ½ tsp
  • Garam masala - 2-3 tsp
  • Fresh ginger, chopped - 4-5 cm
  • Garlic cloves, chopped - 10 no.
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Marinate the beef well.
  2. Cook it in a pressure cooker with 200 ml of water until it becomes soft
  3. If there is water left over after the meat is done, cook with the lid open on a high flame till the water has completely evaporated.
  4. Pour three to four tablespoons of coconut oil and saute two medium-sized onions, slices and a few curry leaves.
  5. Then add the cooked beef into it. Fry on a medium flame until the meat turns brown.
  6. Serve hot.
